Lasagna

Description

When I was child my mom always used to make the best lasagna. Because she was such a narcacist, she never shared that recipe with anyone, including me. She died without ever divulging it to anyone and no one could ever find a record of it anywhere.

Say what you will about the woman but her lasagna was the best lasagna you could have ever tasted in your whole sad, empty life. So, to spite her, I spent years trying to recreate the flavours and exquisite mouth feel of her coveted lasagna and I think this recipe has achieved just that.

So please, use this recipe to help me spread the taste across the globe and rub it into my bitch mom's dead ass face!

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) package lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (16 ounce) jar of spaghetti sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 pound shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 pint ricotta cheese

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). In a large skillet over medium-high heat, brown beef and season with salt and pepper; drain. Stir in spaghetti sauce and garlic and simmer 5 minutes.
  3. In a medium bowl, combine mozzarella, Cheddar and ricotta; stir well. In 9x13 inch pan, alternate layers of noodles, meat mixture and cheese mixture until pan is filled.
  4. Bake in preheated oven for 30 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Say a prayer to my mother, may she burn in hell, and enjoy!
